Learning Plan (Bootcamp)

Why am I here?

My aim is to move into a developer role to start gaining more experience and practice. In the long term I want to give myself more freedom to move around/travel. I would like to move back into financial services, but not working for a bank or another large company. I want to help to provide services that are not currently accessible to everyday people and that benefit them. This might even come in the form of me starting something myself/with others. However I am not locked into this idea, if another opportunity that allows me to apply my newly developed skills arises, I might move into another area. I just enjoy working on interest technology and concepts with a great moral philosphy behind it. I really want to be in a situation that challenges me and is interesting.

What is going to help and hinder my learning?

I still think communication, especially in the online format will be the biggest challenge, reminding myself to stay engaged with other learners even if I am not directly working with them.

My strengths which are problem solving, resourcefulness, persistence and teamwork will still help me through the bootcamp. I have been able to utilise these consistently through the foundations material. I am struggling with the teamwork aspect over the online format so I will need to make an effort to attend any extra events and reach out to people to get to know everyone.

During foundations I have also realised that I have a major weakness in the design area. A lack of confidence / enthusiasm has meant that I have found myself working more on the functionality of projects rather than how they visually work and interact. I have a couple of thoughts about this. I could either lean into the functionality aspect and specialise more on that side of things, or I could try to work on the visual side a little harder. The best option I think is to not care how things look, but still try things visually to make them interesting and find a way to get engaged with the visual side of things.

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be

It is hard to decide whether it might be the communication side of things or just keeping it together for 9 weeks and giving every part enough attention. I hope that with daily classes and pair programming, meet-ups the social side will develop as I am not a huge IM person.

That means I think the biggest challenge will be sustaining effort for the whole course. I don’t usually have any issues with focus once I get stuck into a task, it’s that I can draw out the breaks too long, or if I get distracted it goes for a while. Writing this it is clear that I will just need to focus on staying focused, limiting breaks, and putting in extra effort when starting tasks to get in the flow.

What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I would like to work on leadership, reflection and collaboration. Leadership is pretty straightforward, I have not seeked out leadership roles in recent years and that needs to change. Reflection is going to consist of two focuses, consistency and action. Consistency is just making sure to reflect every day, and after each task. Action is making sure that I review my reflections and am acting upon my insights and suggestions. Finally collaboration, that is more complex as I enjoy working in groups and seek group situations out, I just think I can work on weaving my ideas with others ideas.

What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

My expectations are the same as before. I expect the Bootcamp team to be helpful and available. If I need advice on material I should go to others in my cohort first, but if I still need advice I expect the Bootcamp team will help. I expect for the assignments and key events to be clearly communicated, and I expect the facilitators to help to nurture a valuable learning environment for all students.

Additionally, I do ask for understanding from the Bootcamp team when shit happens, or if I get behind.

What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp

Again I wrote my earlier commitments with the bootcamp in mind. To manage my workload I need to firstly make sure I am aware of my time spent working and time constraints. I need to make sure to use my calendar to block out time for certain tasks. When the bootcamp starts to ramp up, I know what I need to do to stay in the right headspace to succeed. My only priorities outside of class are spending time with my partner, exercising (running during the week, and surfing on the weekend) and occasionally catching up with friends or classmates for the odd drink or dinner. I will focus on actively practising pomodoro breaks, they provide a healthier working pattern. They also allow me to know when I have been stuck for too long on a problem and could do with an extra hand.



Learning Plan (Foundations)

Why am I here?

My long term goal is to develop my own ideas or help develop other people's ideas I find interesting and valuable. I am not particularly driven to climb a corporate ladder, I am more concerned about working with people that are passionate about what they do and why they do it. I want to earn an income by doing something that I find both challenging and interesting. I want flexibility and freedom. I have thought previously that I might enjoy a back-end focused role, but I am open to trying things out and seeing what I enjoy the most.

What is going to help and hinder my learning?

My key strengths are problem solving, resourcefulness, persistence and teamwork. The first three should help me to find the solution to most problems I encounter, however I need to wary about myself either struggling on a problem for too long before asking for help or creating a hacked together solution that works (maybe) but does not follow best practice and is confusing to others (possibly also to later me). I hope my enjoyment of working in teams will help to make anytime spent collaborating a valuable learning experience.

I need to really push myself to be a self-starter, someone that leaps forward at challenges and embraces the possibilities of an assignment or idea. If I can work on this skill, the learning experience at dev academy, and connections made here will be so much more valuable. I also need to make sure that I am communicating effectively and engaged with the cohort, especially during foundations, to allow me to reap the benefits that sharing and collaboration can have on learning. I would like to work on being more open and sharing with my peers regarding my work and ideas. In doing this I still need to make sure that everyone gets a chance to share, encouraging less forthcoming people to share their ideas.

What will I do during this programme?

To manage my workload I need to firstly make sure I am aware of my time spent working and time constraints. I need to make sure to use my calendar to block out time for certain tasks. When the bootcamp starts to ramp up, I know what I need to do to stay in the right headspace to succeed. My only priorities outside of class are spending time with my partner, exercising (running during the week, and surfing on the weekend) and occasionally catching up with friends or classmates for the odd drink or dinner.

I will focus on actively practising pomodoro breaks, they provide a healthier working pattern. They also allow me to know when I have been stuck for too long on a problem and could do with an extra hand.

What do I want from the facilitation team

I expect the facilitation team to be helpful and available. If I need advice on material I should go to others in my cohort first, but if I still need advice I expect the facilitation team will help. I expect for the assignments and key events to be clearly communicated, and I expect the facilitators to help to nurture a valuable learning environment for all students.
