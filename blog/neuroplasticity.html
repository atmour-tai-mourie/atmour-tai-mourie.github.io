Neuroplasticity

How does an understanding of neuroplasticity benefit people

An understanding of neuroplasticity can have two benefits for a person. Firstly, understanding neuroplasticity allows us to tailor our learning so that it is efficient and successful. We can direct learning in a way that creates the desired pathways in our brain and strengthens them, increasing our learning effectiveness. Secondly, having an understanding of neuroplasticity allows us to bypass the assumptions of fixed ability and intelligence. This allows a learner to take back autonomy of their learning journey. They can build their own learning journeys based on neuroplasticity principles, which may take them further than they previously thought possible.

Harnessing the principles of neuroplasticity for my own benefit

I can use my understanding of neuroplasticity by practising tools over and over in different situations and in different ways. By keeping in mind that I can harness neuroplasticity principles to learn anything I can keep myself motivated in challenging problems and lessons. The keys for me are repetition, variation and determination.

Increasing your neuroplasticity

By trying and persevering with new hobbies, techniques or practises we can build new pathways in our brain. Mindfulness and meditation is a major focus for me in this area. I want to develop my brain so that I can maximise the benefits of these tools. By practising mindfulness everyday, I can build and strengthen the pathways in my brain. We can increase neuroplasticity by practising, learning and persevering through challenging problems and concepts. Such as kata and new web development projects. You can also increase my neuroplasticity through regular exercise and visualisation

What is a growth mindset and why is it relevant

A growth mindset is the concept that intelligence is not fixed, and anything can be learned through persistence. Dweck also demonstrates how understanding this concept itself can be a revolutionary factor in motivating children, as it increases their belief in themselves, while showing them they need to put in hard work to achieve their goals and develop themselves. By extrapolating these findings outward along with other studies Dweck believes a growth mindset can be beneficial to anyone. It is relevant to me as I embark on this challenging journey, during which I will and have already had doubts about my ability for success. By knowing that persistence will allow me to succeed and grow, I can and will proceed with more confidence, and perseverance to push through challenging barriers.

What surprised me when learning about the growth mindset

I found the motivational aspects of the growth mindset interesting, how it can help us to overcome doubt and exceed our expectations.

How will you integrate a growth mindset into your learning journey?

I want to try to be more process oriented, rather than focusing on outcomes. By ensuring that I am practising enough and in the right way, I can grow my ability as a web developer and person. I need to think more about future possibilities rather than what is possible now.
