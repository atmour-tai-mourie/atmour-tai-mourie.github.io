Values and Strengths

My Values

My values revolve around doing what is right for the community, learning continuously and enjoying life. I strongly believe in fairness, and that everybody should be treated equally. When learning I value knowledge along with the open-mindedness and curiosity I believe you need to learn. I believe in pragmatism and being challenged, finding a solution and executing no matter the circumstances. Finally, humour, love and adventure give me drive and happiness, and they are all essential for me and my life.

There is a situation I was in a couple of years ago that challenged my values and made me think about what mattered to me. I was working as a Forex CFD Broker, the company's core business was providing an opportunity for regular people to enter highly leveraged positions on financial products such as currencies, oil or cryptocurrencies. These products meant that our clients could either earn or lose incredible amounts of money in seconds. Many of our partners who referred clients (for a reward) tended to focus on the potential for riches, rather than the losses. Alas, most of the time they lost money, especially the people that couldn’t afford to lose. When they lost, because they had taken their position with us, we collected the money. It was essentially an online casino, under the guise of an investment.

The longer I worked there, and as I moved over to the operational side of the business my naivety subsided and I began to realise how much our clients were losing and where it was going. That is where my values were seriously challenged. In my time at this company I was able to satisfy many of my core values, learning constantly, and I was so curious about how it all worked. I had to regularly come up with pragmatic solutions, and the furious pace and amount of money I was dealing with was exciting. Helping to manage millions of dollars of risk and creating infrastructure to facilitate the trading of multiples more keyed into my sense of adventure. Finally, I made some incredible friends there and the basis of these friendships were built around humour and love.

Then just after the darling Covid had entered our lives, I had to perform some analysis. Covid had caused some major turbulence in financial markets, our clients were bleeding money and the Australian financial regulator was curious. I was tasked with analysing how much our clients had lost over this period. I can’t remember the exact amount but it was in the tens of millions of a week, then the next week a similar amount. I was truly confronted with what I was working to enable. Combined with the knowledge that many of our clients were either problem gamblers or sent to us with the promise of easy money, I realised that I needed to leave. I had known that I couldn’t stay there for too long, but this moment made me realise that I was enabling something that was truly unfair and predatory. I shook my own pride on my value of fairness and many other altruistic values that I had been brought up to have.

So I resigned, not much longer after that incident. I began work at a training organisation helping others learn, but man did I miss the challenge, the excitement and working with my friends. It took me less than at that job to realise I was completely bored and unchallenged, and ready for the next adventure.

My Strengths & Limitations

Throughout my education and career, my strengths have allowed me to succeed in most of the roles I have been in. My resourcefulness and ability to problem solve have allowed me to find and implement solutions when given a goal or problem. I believe strongly in my ability to resolve conflict and bring everyone together for one solution, which I believe has saved my teams a significant amount of time in the discussion phase of projects or ideas as I easily can bring each person’s ideas and objectives together. My good-willed nature has made teamwork something that comes naturally and I have flourished in tight knit teams, but larger groups or organisations have tested my communication skills. I want to work more to become a self-starter, investigating and pursuing ideas with energy and conviction. I believe I could have achieved more in my work and education if I had focused on this earlier. Another key limitation for me has been organised reflection, as I do reflect frequently but the reflections are not recorded and structured in a way that is optimised for my personal development and longer term growth. Finally, a key thing that has helped me a little, but more often held me back is that I dive into problems head first without a thought. While the energy in these moments is powerful, I find myself wasting time making mistakes that could be avoided with planning and forethought.

My astute ability to problem solve and resourcefulness have and will continue to be beneficial to my learning journey. Not only do they aid me in creating solutions for complex and challenging problems, but they are a key source of energy and flow for me. These strengths should give me the energy and confidence to persist through major obstacles. I can’t wait to begin more team and group oriented projects as I hope my strengths in this area will not only help my groups and I to successfully develop interesting ideas, but create a beneficial learning experience for everyone involved. Hopefully I will be able to help resolve any creative differences or issues with team dynamics should they arise.

In my recent job I was part of a team guiding the development of our organisation's Sales CRM system. This project had already been going for a while and the pace had been snail like. We were essentially told to get it moving and get it done. My role in this project has been to provide guidance from a user’s point of view and perform user testing. During our frequent meetings, I took a passive role, this allowed me to identify areas that were holding us up. One key issue was that our development team was reluctant to give completion estimates for features, which I presume was because they were overloaded with work. This really did not work for my manager, who was constantly infuriated by this and let it show. Her reactions were not conducive to us finishing the project any faster. In this situation, I tried to act as a mediator taking a more active role in the meetings, asking for firm plans and dates from our development team, and organising the rollout plan to ensure visibility over whether these were met or not. I had to also play this role in discussions about features where long winded debates over minute details were frequent, so I tried to summarise conflicting ideas to reach acceptable solutions earlier. These approaches did work to get the project moving, just quite not at the expected pace. I think if I was in this situation again, I would have spent more time preparing for these meetings, being more assertive from the start especially during long winded discussions. Hopefully by doing that I could have helped to form an organised and functional team from the start.
